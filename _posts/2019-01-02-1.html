--- layout: post title: "Some online mattress retailers are actually safe" date: 2019-01-02 00:00:00 -0500 categories: story1 content-pattern: html --- I was recently in the market for a new mattress, and, like any normal person, I searched what was available online.  As it turns out, (as of September 2018), there are a number of different online retailers peddling their wares.  A google search today for "mattress" today revealed the following sponsored links in the following exact order:

  1. saatvamattress.com
  2. nectarsleep.com/‎
  3. amazon.com/mattresses‎
  4. bestmattressonline.org/reviews‎

The following are the next 5 unsponsored links, which means their rank in the search result is mostly driven by the weight given to them by google page rank algorithms.

  1. mattressfirm.com/
  2. casper.com/mattresses/
  3. purple.com/mattresses
  4. costco.com/mattresses.html
  5. wayfair.com/bed-bath/cat/mattresses-c414871.html
  6. ikea.com/us/en/catalog/categories/departments/bedroom/Mattresses/

However, my original search process for a mattress did not lead me to purchase the mattress with the highest precedence on search.  Rather, my decision was more influenced by the numerous ads that began to appear on e-commerce and social media platforms. It was truly these ads that played a larger role in my decision making.  User cookies play a larger role than one might originally imagine in world of e-commerce.  

After reading several articles and watching many review videos on youtube, I settled on the fairly well-known Leesa mattress.  My end conclusion at the time of purchase is that most mattresses are about the same but Leesa does have a mission of caring and giving back to the community.  If I were to pay a premium on a mattress, at least some of my cash would go towards helping someone who needed help getting appropriate bedding(or so my reasoning went).  

Alas, it turns out the mattress was not really worth the price tag that I paid for it, and my sleep did not improve noticeably.  Hence, I made a call to the Leesa customer service after giving the new mattress a fair chance (studies and retailers say to give at least 30-60 days).  By answering 2 short questions, and with a hold time of less than 2 minutes, a call time of less than 4 minutes,  I was able to schedule a pick up for my mattress within the next 2 weeks.    There was no pushback of any sorts by the customer representative, and this was refreshingly appreciated. 

I am thoroughly impressed by Leesa's customer service and would recommend anyone in the market for a new mattress to give them a chance if they are swayed by reviews and their own research.  They are, so far, a company that can be trusted.  I will update this post when the mattress has been picked up and my monies refunded.
